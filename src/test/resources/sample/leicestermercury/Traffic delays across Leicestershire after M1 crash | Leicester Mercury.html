Traffic delays across Leicestershire after M1 crash

By Samantha Fisher

    An 18-year-old male driver was taken to hospital after a crash on the M1 this morning.

    The incident, which happened on the southbound carriageway and involved one vehicle, caused traffic chaos and hold-ups around the city and county for more than six hours.

    The teenager, who was the only person in the car, had to be rescued from the vehicle by firefighters.

    He was taken to Queen's Medical Centre, in Nottingham, for treatment to injuries described by police as minor. He was due to be released from hospital this afternoon.

    The incident happened near to Leicester Forest East services, between junctions 21 and 21a, at about 4.20am.

    A police spokeswoman said the central reservation barriers were damaged in the crash.

    Three lanes of the southbound carriageway between junctions 21 and 22 were closed following the incident. The stretch of the A46 approaching the motorway was also closed.

    The closure had knock-on effects on many other roads in Leicestershire, including Ratby Lane, towards the A47 Hinckley Road, in Leicester Forest East.

    There were also long delays around Anstey, along Krefeld Way, in Beaumont Leys, in New Parks Way and in Braunstone Way, Leicester.

    The M1 southbound was re-opened at about 8.15am, following a police investigation and barrier repairs, but the congestion did not clear for some time afterwards.

    At 9am, Area Traffic Control (ATC) reported delays of more than two hours on the A50 between the Markfield area and the outer ring road.

    At 10.30am, ATC said traffic was still struggling on the A50 between Groby and New Parks Way.

    The congestion was cleared by about 11am.

    People in the area went online to warn road users about the delays.

    Alan Radford told fellow Twitter users to avoid using the M1 southbound as there was "gridlock".

    At about 9am, Tavia Alonzo tweeted: "Been on the A50 in Markfield for an hour."

    Jay Ward posted that traffic was "chaotic around all areas leading to M1".
